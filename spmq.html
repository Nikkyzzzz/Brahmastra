  8. Discuss the different steps of preparing the schedule. What are the Inputs and outputs required by Schedule?
    17

    Steps in Preparing a Schedule

    1. Define Activities
      • Inputs: Project Scope Statement, WBS, Organizational Process Assets, Enterprise Environmental Factors
      • Outputs: Activity List, Activity Attributes, Milestone List
    2. Sequence Activities
      • Inputs: Activity List, Activity Attributes, Milestone List, Project Scope Statement, Organizational Process Assets
      • Outputs: Project Schedule Network Diagrams, Updated Activity List and Attributes
    3. Estimate Activity Resources
      • Inputs: Activity List, Activity Attributes, Resource Calendars, RBS, Enterprise Environmental Factors, Organizational Process Assets
      • Outputs: Activity Resource Requirements, Resource Breakdown Structure, Resource Calendar Updates
    4. Estimate Activity Durations
      • Inputs: Activity List, Activity Attributes, Activity Resource Requirements, Resource Calendars, Project Scope Statement, Organizational Process Assets
      • Outputs: Activity Duration Estimates, Basis of Estimates
    5. Develop Schedule
      • Inputs: Activity List, Attributes, Network Diagrams, Resource Requirements, Calendars, Duration Estimates, Scope Statement, Environmental Factors, Organizational Assets
      • Outputs: Project Schedule, Schedule Baseline, Schedule Data, Project Calendar, Management Plan Updates
    6. Control Schedule
      • Inputs: Management Plan, Project Schedule, Work Performance Information, Organizational Process Assets
      • Outputs: Work Performance Measurements, Organizational Assets Updates, Change Requests, Management Plan Updates, Document Updates

    Key Inputs and Outputs

    Inputs:

    • Project Scope Statement: Defines project boundaries and deliverables.
    • WBS: Breakdown of project scope into manageable sections.
    • Organizational Process Assets: Guidelines, historical data, and lessons from past projects.
    • Enterprise Environmental Factors: External and internal factors affecting project execution.
    • Activity List and Attributes: Detailed tasks and their characteristics.
    • Milestone List: Key project milestones.
    • Resource Calendars and Breakdown Structure: Resource availability and categorization.
    • Activity Resource Requirements: Resource needs for activities.
    • Activity Duration Estimates: Time estimates for activities.
    • Project Schedule Network Diagrams: Logical relationships among activities.
    • Work Performance Information: Status of project activities.

    Outputs:

    • Activity List and Attributes: Finalized activities and their details.
    • Milestone List: Finalized milestones.
    • Project Schedule Network Diagrams: Finalized activity sequences.
    • Resource Requirements and Calendar: Finalized resource needs and availability.
    • Activity Duration Estimates: Finalized time estimates.
    • Basis of Estimates: Documentation supporting duration estimates.
    • Project Schedule and Baseline: Detailed timeline and approved schedule.
    • Schedule Data and Project Calendar: Additional schedule information and working days.
    • Work Performance Measurements: Metrics on activity performance.
    • Change Requests: Proposals for schedule modifications.
    • Management Plan and Document Updates: Revisions reflecting schedule changes.

  18. Explain about the Oldham Hackman job characteristic model under the Staffing in Software Projects.
    22
    23
    The Oldham – Hackman job characteristic model Oldham and Hackman suggest that the satisfaction that a job gives is based on five factors, The first three factors make the job 'meaningful' to the person who is doing it:
    •  skill variety: the number of different skills that the job holder has the opportunity to exercise;
    •  task identity: the degree to which your work and its results are identifiable as belonging to you;
    •  task significance: the degree to which your job has an influence on others.
    The other two factors are:
    •  autonomy: the discretion you have about the way that you do the job;
    •  feedback: the information you get back about the results of your work.
